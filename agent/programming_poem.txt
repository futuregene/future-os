A Poem About Programming

In silent glow of monitor's light,
I chase the bugs throughout the night.
A semicolon out of place,
A missing brace, a null in space.

The compiler speaks in cryptic tongue,
Of errors left when code is young.
But one by one, the warnings fade,
As careful changes have been made.

The loop that once ran wild and free,
Now counts its steps obediently.
The function that returned a mess,
Now does its job with finesse.

Abstraction stacks in layered grace,
Each module finding its own place.
From lowly bits to RESTful calls,
The grand cathedral slowly falls

Into a structure, clean and tight,
Where every piece reflects the light.
Of patterns learned through years of toil,
And midnight oil that seems to boil.

For in this craft of ones and zeros,
We build new worlds, like brave explorers.
With keyboard as our magic wand,
We shape the future and beyond.

Yet when the final build runs green,
The purest joy is felt, unseen.
For in each line of code we write,
A spark of intellect takes flight.
To make the impossible routine.
