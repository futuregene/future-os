# Settings

Open **Settings** from the gear at the bottom of the left sidebar. There's also a **Models** shortcut under *New Chat* that jumps straight to the model list. Settings has three pages: **General**, **Providers**, and **Models**.

## General

Desktop-level options, such as auto-approval behavior. Adjust these to match how hands-on you want to be.

## Providers — where your models come from

### FutureGene (built-in)

The built-in provider. Click **Connect** to sign in:

1. Your system browser opens to authorize the login.
2. If it doesn't open automatically, the app shows a **verification code** and a **copyable link** — open the link in any browser and enter the code.
3. Once authorized, you're connected.

After connecting you can **re-login** or **log out** from this screen. Sign-in happens entirely in the app.

### Custom providers

Add your own OpenAI-compatible or Anthropic-compatible provider. For each one you provide:

- an **id** and **name**,
- the **API type** (OpenAI- or Anthropic-compatible),
- the **Base URL**,
- your **API key**,
- and the **models** it offers.

The app validates these fields and checks that ids are unique. You can edit or remove custom providers later.

> Your provider API keys are part of your app/model settings and are kept separate from any data-source credentials.

## Models — choose what's available

The Models page lists every model the agent can currently use, **grouped by provider** (shown by provider name). You can:

- toggle each model's **visibility**, and
- **search** to find one quickly.

The model selector in the message box draws from this same list, and always shows which provider a model comes from — so picking a model is consistent whether you do it in Settings or mid-conversation.
